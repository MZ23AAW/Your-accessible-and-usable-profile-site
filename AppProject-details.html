Building an App - Medication and Disability Parking Tracker

Project Description:

During my first year at the University of Hertfordshire, I created this app. The objective was to develop an application that would assist users with two essential tasks: finding accessible parking spots and managing their medications/appointments. It sought to solve practical issues that persons with disabilities frequently encounter, making sure they have the resources necessary to obtain both necessary medical care and mobility aids.

Features:

  • Medication tracking with notifications to remind users when to take their medication.
  • During the project's early phases, I researched the key aspects needed to create a user-friendly GUI and assure effective design. To understand how the GUI should function, we used the usability document developed by our team leader as a starting point. I designed a logo,  GP appointment schedule page and an emergency contact form so that my colleagues could see how the GUI would look.
  • Location base searches for disability parking spaces that are nearby.
  • User-friendly interface designed for ease of use by individuals with varying levels of tech literacy.

Role in the Project:

  • Graphical User Interface Designer
  • Researcher
